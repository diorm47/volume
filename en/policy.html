Use of cookies and other tracking mechanisms

To improve your interaction with our Platform, we and our third-party service providers use cookies and other tracking mechanisms to collect information about your usage. Here's how we use these technologies:

  • Cookies are alphanumeric identifiers that are stored on your computer's hard drive through your web browser. They help us improve navigation, speed up login processes and track your actions on our Platform. We use both session and persistent cookies for various purposes.

  • Session cookies: These cookies only exist during your online session and disappear when you close your browser. They allow us to uniquely identify you, process your transactions and confirm your identity while navigating our Platform.

  • Persistent cookies: These cookies remain on your computer after you close your browser. We use them to track aggregate and statistical information about user activity. Please note that our systems currently do not respond to "do not track" signals.

  • Disabling cookies: Most web browsers accept cookies automatically, but you can change your browser settings to block them in the future. The general settings of most browsers allow you to prohibit new cookies, receive notifications when new cookies are received, or disable cookies completely. However, disabling cookies may restrict your access to certain areas of our Platform.

  • Third-party analytics: We use automated devices and applications, such as Google Analytics, Yandex Metrica, etc., to evaluate the use of our Platform. These tools help us improve performance and user experience. Although these third-party analytics providers may use cookies and other tracking technologies, we do not share your personal information with them.

  3. Operating system and software updates. Update your operating systems, antivirus software, and firewalls with the latest security patches or newer versions.

  4. File and printer sharing. Disable file and printer sharing on your computers, especially when connected to the Internet.

  5. Regular backups. Make regular backups of important data to protect against data loss.

  6. Encryption technology. Consider using encryption technology to protect sensitive or sensitive information.

  7. Log out and clear the browser cache: Log out completely and clear the browser cache after each online session on the Platform.

  8. Software and Email Warning. Do not install software or run programs from unknown sources. Delete unwanted or chained emails and refrain from opening email attachments from strangers.

  9. Online Security Practices: Do not disclose personal, financial, or credit card information on obscure or suspicious websites. Avoid using untrusted computers or devices, including public computers or computers in Internet cafes, to access online services or perform financial transactions.
